GLASU: A Communication-Efficient Algorithm for Federated Learning with Vertically Distributed Graph Data

Introducing a model-splitting method and communication-efficient algorithm for training graph neural networks through Vertical Federated Learning (VFL).

What is GLASU?

GLASU proposes a model-splitting method and communication-efficient algorithm to train a graph neural network through Vertical Federated Learning (VFL), where each client owns a part of the node features and a different edge set.

Why is GLASU important?

The current research in VFL focuses on independent samples, but GLASU addresses graph-structured data by splitting the model across the clients and the server. GLASU reduces communication overhead through lazy aggregation and stale updates, achieving convergence guarantees and matching the accuracy of centralized training.
