Revolutionizing Exoplanet Detection with Deep Learning

Traditional methods of detecting exoplanets in radial velocity data are time-consuming and prone to false positives due to instrument errors and noise from the star. ExoplANNET is a deep learning algorithm that is trained to classify planetary signals in the radial velocity data. It performs better than traditional methods, with fewer false positives and a faster execution time.

How ExoplANNET Works

ExoplANNET is a deep learning algorithm that uses neural networks to classify planetary signals in radial velocity data. The algorithm is trained on a large dataset of simulated data, and is able to detect planetary signals with high accuracy and low false positive rates. The algorithm is designed to be fast and efficient, enabling astronomers to process large amounts of data quickly and easily.

Pricing

ExoplANNET is offered as a subscription-based service to astronomers and researchers. The service includes access to the algorithm, training and support, and data analysis tools. The pricing is based on the volume of data processed by the algorithm, with discounts available for long-term subscriptions and large volumes of data.
